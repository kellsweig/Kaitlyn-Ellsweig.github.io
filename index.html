The Friends of the Cobbossee Watershed

Combining machine learning and data analysis to provide insights to nonprofit organization

I worked with The Friends of the Cobbossee Watershed, an environmental nonprofit working to protect the region's waterbodies from pollution and the threat of invasive species. I first used machine learning to develop a Gradient Boost model to predict the number of Courtesy Boat Inspections the organization would conduct in different scenarios. I then created multiple scenarios and deployed the model on those scenarios to create labor and budget projections. Using these projections and other analysis, I drafted recommendations for changes to budget and staffing for 2024.

Reddit Post Classification Project

Utilizing NLP and Machine Learning to Classify Posts

In this project I developed a classfication model that was able to determine with 99.6% accuracy which of two related subreddits a post came from. First, I used the Reddit API, PRAW, to collect reddit posts from the subreddits r/ultralight and r/camping. I used a TF-IDF vectorizer for NLP and then trained a Random Forest, Classifier, AdaBoost Classifier and Voting Classifier to achieve my final model.

US Covid-19 Outcomes Analsysis

As the project manager of a group within a Data Science Immersive Program, I led a comprehensive data science project focused on understanding outcomes of the Covid-19 pandemic and formulating a resiliency plan for future pandemics. My team conducted an extensive analysis utilizing a combination of machine learning and other data analysis techniques. We explored a myriad of variables, from policy considerations to demographics, aiming to improve Covid-19 response strategies and devise a robust resiliency plan. Leading the team through various stages, from data collection and cleaning to model development and interpretation, I ensured effective collaboration and cohesive progress. The diverse set of models, including white-box linear regressions and black-box models such as Extra Trees and Random Forests, provided nuanced insights into the factors influencing Covid-19 outcomes at both state and county levels. This project not only showcased my leadership skills but also demonstrated a keen understanding of the intricate relationships within public health data, contributing valuable insights for future pandemic preparedness.

Predicting Housing Prices in Ames Iowa

In this project, I was assigned to develop a predictive model for housing prices in Ames, IA and was limited in model selection to OLS. Employing multiple linear regression models, I explored various features such as house type, square footage, and quality of materials. Through extensive exploratory data analysis, model development, and refinement, I successfully achieved a high-performing model with an R-squared value of 0.905 for validation data. I utilized techniques such as feature engineering and regularization to enhance model accuracy. This project showcases my proficiency in data analysis, model development, and strategic decision-making, providing actionable insights for real-world scenarios in the realm of construction and real estate.
